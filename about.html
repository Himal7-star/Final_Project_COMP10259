Wild Salmon

Wild Salmon found in both freshwater and saltwater habitats. Born in freshwater streams, they migrate to the ocean before returning to spawn in their original streams. Omega-3 fatty acids give the fruit its high nutritional value and flavorful, pink flesh. vital to the health of the ecosystem and the support of different species.

Salmon Brown Trout

Freshwater salmonid species known as the brown trout (Salmo trutta). Olive-brown to silver coloration with black and red spots on the sides. Originally from Europe, but spread to many other places, including North America. prefers lakes, rivers, and streams with crystal-clear, chilly water. Anglers prize it for its uncertainly and power in battle.

Salmon Chinook

The biggest species of salmon in the Pacific is also known as the "King salmon." distinctive appearance with a back that is blue-green and sides that may be silver with black spots. average weight is between 20 and 50 pounds, but some people weigh more than 100 pounds. The North Pacific Ocean, from California to Alaska, the Bering Sea, and Japan and Russia are all included in the range. Significant for both commercial and recreational fishing, highly prized for large size and rich flavour.

Salmon Coho

Already referred to as silver salmon. Black spots on the back and tail, with a distinctive silver-blue coloration. averages 6 to 12 pounds in weight, but can weigh up to 30 pounds. found in the rivers along North America's western coast and the North Pacific Ocean. known for its flavour and fighting prowess when used as game fish.

Salmon Pink

Pink salmon is also known as Humpy salmon. The smallest and most numerous Pacific salmon species. Large black oval spots on the back and tail give it a distinct appearance. Average weight is 3 to 5 pounds, and length is about two feet. It has a two-year life cycle and is harvested commercially for its meat.
